Publication Ethics and Malpractice Statement

The ICA Regional Hub Thailand 2026 Proceedings is committed to maintaining the highest standards of academic integrity, transparency, and ethical publication practices. Authors, reviewers, and editors are expected to adhere to the following principles throughout the submission, review, and publication process.

Responsibilities of Authors

Originality and Prior Publication
Authors must submit original work that has not been previously published in substantially the same form in another proceedings volume, journal, or edited publication. Any overlap with previously published or presented work must be clearly disclosed.

Plagiarism and Proper Citation
All sources, quotations, ideas, data, images, and materials derived from the work of others must be properly acknowledged and cited. Plagiarism, including self-plagiarism and unattributed use of generative AI outputs, is strictly prohibited.

Data Integrity and Research Ethics
Authors are responsible for ensuring the accuracy and integrity of their research. Fabrication, falsification, manipulation, or misrepresentation of data, findings, methods, or sources is considered a serious breach of academic ethics.

Authorship
All listed authors must have made a significant contribution to the research and approve the final version submitted for publication.

Use of Artificial Intelligence
Authors may use AI-assisted tools for language editing, formatting, or research support. However, authors remain fully responsible for the accuracy, originality, and integrity of all submitted content. AI systems may not be listed as authors.

Responsibilities of Reviewers

Reviewers should accept assignments only when they have the appropriate expertise and no conflict of interest. All manuscripts must be treated as confidential and may not be shared or used for personal purposes. Reviews should be conducted objectively, fairly, and professionally, focusing on the scholarly quality and relevance of the work. Reviewers are also expected to complete their evaluations within the designated timeframe and promptly notify the Editorial Team of any anticipated delays.

Responsibilities of Editors and the Proceedings Committee

Fair Evaluation
Submissions will be assessed solely on their academic merit, originality, relevance, and contribution to knowledge, without discrimination based on nationality, ethnicity, gender, religion, institutional affiliation, political views, or personal characteristics.

Confidentiality
Editors and members of the Proceedings Committee shall maintain the confidentiality of all submitted manuscripts, review reports, and editorial communications.

Editorial Independence
Editorial decisions shall be made independently and based on scholarly considerations. Acceptance for conference presentation does not guarantee publication in the proceedings.

Ethical Violations and Misconduct

Allegations of plagiarism, data fabrication, duplicate publication, authorship disputes, unethical research practices, or other forms of misconduct will be investigated by the Proceedings Editorial Team and Scientific Committee. Authors will be given an opportunity to respond to any allegations. Depending on the severity of the violation, actions may include:

  • Rejection of the submission
  • Withdrawal of the paper from the review process
  • Retraction of a published paper
  • Notification to the author's institution where appropriate
  • Exclusion from future ICA Regional Hub Thailand publication opportunities
Important Note

All papers published in the ICA Regional Hub Thailand 2026 Proceedings must acknowledge that they were presented at and reviewed through ICA Regional Hub Thailand 2026, an official concurrent regional event of the International Communication Association (ICA).

Papers published in these proceedings should not be described as papers accepted to or presented at the ICA Annual Conference unless separate acceptance has been obtained through the ICA Annual Conference submission process.
